SALES UPLIFT DASHBOARD — KEY INSIGHTS
=======================================
(Based on RetailTransactions.csv — 20,000 transactions, 3,981 customers, ₹555.60M total sales)

1. Balanced regional performance, East leads narrowly.
   Sales are almost evenly split across regions (25.4% East, 25.2% South, 24.8% West,
   24.6% North). East is the top performer (~₹141.3M) but no region dominates —
   growth strategies should be nationally consistent rather than region-specific.

2. Grocery and Electronics are the top-earning categories.
   Grocery (₹95.0M) and Electronics (₹93.9M) lead category sales, closely followed
   by Beauty (₹93.2M) and Sports (₹93.1M). Furniture trails as the weakest category
   (₹88.1M), suggesting an opportunity to boost furniture promotions or bundling.

3. Offline still edges out Online sales, but the gap is narrow.
   Offline channel contributes 50.6% (₹281.1M) vs Online's 49.4% (₹274.5M) — a near
   50/50 split. This indicates a healthy omnichannel presence with room to grow
   online sales through targeted digital campaigns.

4. High average order value indicates strong basket sizes.
   With ~20K transactions generating ₹555.6M, the average order value is roughly
   ₹27,780 per transaction — suggesting customers are buying in bulk or purchasing
   higher-value items rather than making frequent small purchases.

5. Sales peak in October and December, dip in February.
   October (₹49.7M) and December (₹47.7M) are the strongest months, likely driven by
   festive/holiday shopping season, while February is consistently the weakest month
   (₹40.9M) — a good candidate for promotional campaigns to smooth seasonal dips.

6. Top-selling products span daily essentials and lifestyle categories.
   Wheat Flour (₹14.6M), Sunscreen (₹14.4M), Badminton Racket (₹14.4M), and Shampoo
   (₹14.4M) are the best-selling products — showing strong demand for both everyday
   staples and discretionary lifestyle/sports items.

7. Payment methods are fairly evenly distributed, UPI slightly ahead.
   UPI (₹141.0M) is marginally the most-used payment mode, followed closely by Cash
   (₹140.5M), Credit Card (₹138.3M), and Net Banking (₹135.8M) — indicating diverse
   and well-adopted payment preferences across the customer base with no single
   dominant method.
